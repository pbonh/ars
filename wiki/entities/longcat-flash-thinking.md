## Overview

LongCat-Flash-Thinking-2601 is a reasoning-capable large language model developed by the Meituan LongCat Team (Wang et al., 2026). It is cited in the Heavy Skill paper as one of the models demonstrating promising results through heavy thinking decomposition — breaking reasoning into parallel generation followed by sequential deliberation.

## Characteristics

- **Developer**: Meituan LongCat Team (same group that authored the Heavy Skill paper)
- **Architecture**: Reasoning model with native heavy thinking support
- **Relationship to Heavy Skill**: Serves as an in-house implementation of the two-stage reasoning paradigm
- **Ecosystem**: Part of the broader LongCat model family alongside LongCat-Flash-Prover

## Common Strategies

- Referenced as empirical evidence that heavy thinking patterns can be built natively into model architecture
- Used alongside Kimi K2 and PaCoRe to show the pattern's emergence across different organizations
- Represents Meituan's investment in advanced reasoning capabilities for agentic applications
