## Overview

**Michael Nygard** is a software architect and author widely credited with popularizing the concept of Architectural Decision Records. His 2011 blog post *"Documenting Architecture Decisions"* introduced a lightweight, numbered Markdown format that many teams still use as their default ADR template.

## Characteristics

- **Practitioner-author**: Wrote *Release It!* (2007) and other works on resilient systems.
- **Influential blogger**: The 2011 Cognitect blog post is frequently cited as the origin of modern ADR practice.
- **Template legacy**: His original ADR template (title, context, decision, consequences, status) became the basis for numerous derivatives.

## Common Strategies

- Teams starting ADR adoption often begin with Nygard's minimal template and later evolve to richer formats (e.g., MADR or Y-statement) as their needs mature.
- Nygard's emphasis on *consequences* (both positive and negative) is a pattern adopted by almost all subsequent templates.
