## Overview

**Hari Krishnan** is the author of the [[entities/intent-driven-dev|intent-driven.dev]]
blog post "Architectural Decision Records with Spec-Driven Development using
OpenSpec" (2026-04-29), which argues for keeping architectural rationale durable
by storing [[concepts/architectural-decision-record|ADRs]] outside the OpenSpec
change folder. Beyond authorship of this post, little is recorded here — this page
is a low-confidence stub created from a single source.

## Characteristics

- **Author / advocate** of intent-driven development's "keep the reasoning close
  to the code" principle.
- **Associated work:** the [[concepts/spec-driven-with-adr-schema|`spec-driven-with-adr`]]
  OpenSpec schema and the [[entities/openspec-schemas|openspec-schemas]] repo.

## Common Strategies

- Frames a tooling change (a custom OpenSpec schema) with a concrete failure story
  — the PostgreSQL-vs-DynamoDB rationale lost at archival — to motivate
  [[concepts/durable-artifacts-vs-scaffolding|making rationale durable]].
