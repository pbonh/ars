## Overview

**Michael Keeling** is a software architect and researcher known for advancing the practice of architectural decision documentation in both academia and industry. He co-authored the IEEE Software article *"Love Unrequited: The Story of Architecture, Agile, and How Architecture Decision Records Brought Them Together"* (2022) and has presented empirical findings on ADR usage.

## Characteristics

- **Pragmatic researcher**: Bridges academic rigor with practitioner concerns.
- **IEEE contributor**: His Pragmatic Designer column article is a frequently cited reference on the intersection of agile and architecture.
- **Presenter**: Delivered "Architecture Decision Records in Action" with Joe Runde (IBM), sharing empirical numbers on adoption and value.

## Common Strategies

- Engineering leaders cite Keeling's work when arguing that ADRs are compatible with agile rather than antithetical to it.
- His empirical framing (numbers on adoption, measurable benefits) is useful for building the business case for ADR tooling investment.
